🇵🇰 Shipped: "My Pakistan Travel Diary" — A Mental Wellness + Travel App

I just completed a portfolio project that combines two passions: travel and mental health support.

**What I Built:**
A desktop application that transforms travel documentation into a gateway for emotional connection and peer support. Built in 1 week with C++17, SFML, SQLite, and HTML/CSS export.

**Core Features:**
✅ Desktop app with SFML GUI - Add, edit, delete travel entries with photos
✅ SQLite database - Store travel memories with mood tracking
✅ 6-mood tracking system (Peaceful, Joyful, Reflective, Nostalgic, Energized, Anxious)
✅ Pre-trip mental wellness questionnaire - Support flagging & helpline integration
✅ Multi-page professional website export - City guides, travel tips, safety info
✅ YouTube videos embedded - Real travel vlogs for each destination
✅ Newspaper & magazine links - Guardian, Express Tribune, Lonely Planet features
✅ Interactive OpenStreetMap - Free maps, no API key needed
✅ Light/Dark mode toggle - Professional responsive design
✅ 8 real travel photos - From my actual Pakistan travels

**Live Website:** https://MaazGul-2006.github.io/MyPakistanDiary-Website/

**GitHub:** https://github.com/MaazGul-2006/MyPakistanDiary

**Why This Matters:**
Many Pakistani university students struggle with depression & anxiety silently. By embedding wellness check-ins within a travel diary, this app normalizes emotional expression and creates safe spaces for peer support—using the "Wisdom of Crowds" concept to aggregate community insights without centralization.

**Technical Highlights:**
- OOP design with clean class hierarchy
- SQLite CRUD operations + aggregation queries
- SFML event handling & GUI rendering
- HTML/CSS responsive design
- Python-based multi-page website generator
- GitHub Pages deployment
- Open-source & fully documented

**The Mood Tracking System:**
Every entry captures emotional state at that moment, creating a personal wellness timeline showing how different places affect mental health. See patterns like:
- "Faisal Mosque always makes me feel Peaceful"
- "Mountain hikes energize me when anxious"
- "Food Street brings out my Joyful side"

When marking a trip as "Planned", users answer 4 wellness questions:
- Traveling alone?
- How are you feeling?
- Does anyone know your plans?
- Energy level?

If flagged for concern → Display Umang helpline: 0317-4288665

## 🚀 Roadmap: Building a Community

The vision extends beyond personal journaling:

**Phase 1: Real-Time Community Sync**
A web platform where travelers across Pakistan can share their diaries, creating collective insights:
- Backend API syncing with desktop app
- User authentication & profiles (anonymous option)
- Real-time entry sharing & collaboration
- Community leaderboard of top-rated places

**Phase 2: Collective Wellness Intelligence**
When 100 travelers log their moods at Faisal Mosque:
- 80% feel Peaceful ✅
- 15% feel Reflective
- 5% feel Anxious

New travelers see: "Most people feel Peaceful here" — organic safety crowdsourcing without centralized data.

**Phase 3: Campus Integration**

**Phase 5: University & Healthcare Partnership**

## 💡 Why This Matters at National Scale

Pakistan's mental health crisis is **real & urgent**:
- 34% university students report depression (Dawn News, 2023)
- Massive stigma around seeking help
- Limited counseling resources
- Isolation compounds the problem

Travel apps are common. **But a travel app with mental wellness built-in?** That's different.

This platform:
✅ **Normalizes emotional expression** through shared travel experiences
✅ **Creates safe peer communities** without judgment
✅ **Aggregates wisdom** showing "you're not alone"
✅ **Connects to professional help** when needed
✅ **Provides data** to mental health organizations for better resource allocation
